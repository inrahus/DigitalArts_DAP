Bachelor of Arts: Digital Arts

The BA in Digital Arts is a 4 Year Professional Degree that allows you to tailor your specialisation, and also gives you a useful, broad skillset. It is an Honors-equivalent that combines the artistic, technical and critical skills that equip you to be an innovator, practical problem-solver, as well as a critical thinker and artist.

    Your first year, and what is know as the Anologue year, includes core academic and professional concepts such as:
  • Game Design
  • Storytelling & Drawing
  • Critical Art Analysis

    And from second year, when we move into digital development, we also address:
  • Programming
  • 3D Modelling & Animation

    Once you move into the 3rd and 4th year levels of study, you continue with critical theory, and choose your specialisations from a range of professional majors, namely:
  • Game Design
  • Animation
  • Writing
  • Interactive Media

    • In the senior years, you will be able to experiment with growing technologies like VR and AR, and produce large-scale creative projects. We will be sending you off with a broad portfolio with which to enter industry.

    Application Requirements:
  • Minimum APS of 34
  • Minimum of 60% for English as a Home Language OR English as a First Additional Language

    • While Mathematics is not a requisite, it will be considered as part of your application. The Digital Arts degree involves coding for all students and Mathematics can be an valuable part of the applicant’s application.

    Additional Requirements:
  • Questionnaire:
    The questionnaire will ask a series of questions. Some of these are about you as an applicant and some of them pertain to the kinds of work we do at Wits Digital Arts.

      You will be assessed based on your ability to:
    • Problem-Solve
    • Engage Critically

      You must submit the following with your questionnaire:
    • 1-page CV, detailing your personal information - particularly your:
      • Full Name
      • Address
      • Telephone or cellphone contact information
      • Email contact information
      • Name of the last school you attende
      • Year of matriculation (or expected matriculation).

    • Copy of your ID

    • A transcript of your most recent marks. This being a certified copy of your:
      • Final Grade 11 mark
      • Matric Certificate
      • Academic transcript

    • A Critical Essay (1-2 pages)
      In a short essay (1-2 pages) discuss a game, animation or interactive media (digital or analogue) that you have recently engaged with. Your essay should not be a description. It should critically engage with how the artwork works, the themes and the effect that this has on the user/player/audience. You should be able to describe what you liked or did not like. You should identify one to two aspects of the artwork that you found most or least effective and discuss why that is.

    • A Portfolio of Work
      This portfolio can be on any creative work that you have completed. You are free to submit drawings, animations, games, photography, creative writing or any work that you have done. This is a moment for you to show off your abilities and talents. Feel free to submit whatever you feel highlights you best.


    • Questionnaires will be first issued in May 2021. We will continue to issue to new applicants every two weeks until November. The final deadline for the questionnaire is 19th November 2021.

    If you have not received a questionnaire 3 weeks after your application, please contact Liezel Borman (Liezel.Borman@wits.ac.za) to request that it is resent to you.

Wait-listing

Applicants with an entry requirement of at least 30-33 APS points are wait-listed, subject to place availability.

Additional Selection Criteria

You will be required to attend a digital arts workshop.
Due to limited space, meeting the minimum requirements does not guarantee a place. Final selection is made subject to place availability, academic results, and other entry requirements, where applicable.

Bachelor of Science in Engineering: Digital Arts

The BEngSc in Digital Arts is a 3 Year Degree in the School of Electrical and Information Engineering including subjects from Digital Arts. The combination of information engineering and creative production gives you a broad range of critical and technical skills. This equips you to enter the technology sector with an unique perspective: blending software development, systems thinking, and creative application.

The BEngSc Digital Arts has a set curriculum. Subjects taken in the School of Electrical and Information Engineering include Maths, Physics, Mechanics, Electric Circuits, Microprocessors, Software Development, Data Structures and Algorithms amongst others. In Digital Arts the focus is on Game Design and Interactive Media.

After you’ve graduated, you can enter industry or continue into the professional BSc Degree in Electrical and Information Engineering. You can also opt to complete your BA Honours degree, in which you will be able to experiment with growing technologies like VR and AR, and produce large-scale creative projects.

Post-Graduate Offers


Honours

The BA Honours combines practical components with research and is available on a bespoke, modular course arrangement that allows for areas of specialisation. Applicants to the programme will be required to complete a compulsory theory subject, a research project or essay, the Digital Arts Project (a creative practical major in which students write their own briefs and work with mentors over a semester to geenrate work in their field of interest) and a choice of one other practical subjects. Applicants can choose from Animation (2D OR 3D OR Experimental), Interactive Media, Writing and Interactivity or Advanced Game Design (the latter requires prior experience in Game Design).

Application Requirements:
Honours: A Bachelor’s Degree within a related field (e.g. Software Development, Graphic Design, Architecture) with an overall aggregate of 65% for practical subjects, as well as 65% theory. Applicants will be requested to supply a critical writing sample as well as practical portfolio.



Masters & PhD

The Masters degree is aimed at students with a strongly articulated research interest. Research can be produced as written academic research or as a combination of written research and practice research. All applications are dependent on supervisor availability, so potential candidates are urged to identify a supervisor and consult with them before making an application. The two research path include:

1. Purely academic research, in which the candidate is expected to produce only a written dissertation.

2. Combination of academic and practice research. in which the candidate is expected to produce a smaller written dissertation along with a body of practical work. The weighting can be either 50 / 50 or 60 /40, dependent on the scope of the project. The dissertation should provide a theoretical framework as well as an analysis of the practical work produced. A permanent record of the practical production must accompany the dissertation.

The areas of research which are available include:

Animation
Research topics for Animation include animation history, theory, practice and production systems, or animation education and various forms of motion studies.

Interactive Media
Research topics in Interactive Media include both practice and theory in these areas: interactivity and creative / arts practice, interactivity and development, digital innovation, social media, mobile media, user-focused design or South African and African digital media development and theory.

Game Design
Research topics for Game Design include explorations of both practice and theory in the areas: game design, game in culture, games in education, and interactive writing.

Experimental Narratives
Research topics in Experimental Narratives incorporate a wide variety of topics, from performance in writing, alternative narratives, non-linear narratives and even AI, computer generated texts.

Applicants need to hold an Honours or Four Year Professional degree (NQF Level 8).
